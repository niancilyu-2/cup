Rules & Scoring

The game

A pick'em bracket for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. Predict who advances through every round, earn points for getting it right, and ride the leaderboard from June 11 to the final on July 19.

How to play (two stages)

Stage 1 — Group standings. Open now, locks at first WC kickoff on June 11.

  1. Pick your name (or add yourself) on the home page.
  2. For each of the 12 groups, predict who finishes 1st and 2nd.
  3. Hit Submit picks when you're done — you can keep editing until June 11.

Stage 2 — Bracket. Opens automatically once group stage ends and the real R32 pairings are confirmed. Locks at first R32 kickoff on June 28.

  1. The R32 matches are pre-filled with the actual qualifying teams (24 group winners/runners-up + 8 best 3rd-place wildcards, per FIFA's rules).
  2. Click winners through R32 → R16 → QF → SF → Final, plus the 3rd-place match.
  3. Enter your tiebreaker: how many goals will the eventual champion score across the entire tournament?
  4. Optionally, predict exact final scores for any knockout match for bonus points.
  5. Submit when you're done — editable until R32 kickoff.

Deadlines

  • Group picks lock: Thursday, June 11, 2026 — 1:00 PM Mexico City time (Mexico vs South Africa, Estadio Azteca).
  • Bracket picks lock: Sunday, June 28, 2026 — 3:00 PM Pacific time (first R32 match, SoFi Stadium).

After each lock, no more edits to that section. Everyone's picks become visible for that stage.

How the bracket cascade works

The bracket is one continuous tree. R32 starts from the real qualifiers; once you pick a winner, that team flows into the R16 slot they're due to play in. R16 winners feed QF, QF feeds SF, SF feeds the Final (and the SF losers play for 3rd place).

Change a winner partway through and downstream slots update automatically. Any winner pick whose team is no longer in its match is quietly dropped — you'll need to re-pick that match.

The tiebreaker, explained

If two players finish on equal points, the player whose tiebreaker number is closest to the actual champion's total tournament goals wins.

One key thing: the prediction is about the real champion, not the team you picked to win. If you predict "Brazil will win and score 22 goals" but Argentina actually lifts the trophy with 14 goals, your tiebreaker number (22) is compared against 14 (off by 8). Pick a realistic number for whoever you think wins.

Visibility

Until first kickoff on June 11, only your own picks are visible to you. No peeking at how other players are filling their brackets. At kickoff, everything is revealed and the leaderboard goes live.

Picks model: details

  • Group picks: save partial progress (just 1st without 2nd) at any time.
  • R32 pairings: set by FIFA's bracket-resolution rules after group stage — you don't draft teams into slots.
  • Winner picks: click a team in a match to advance them; click again to un-pick.
  • Exact-score bonuses: optional. Predict 2-1, 3-0, etc. for any knockout match. Earn +2 if your score matches the real final score exactly.

How results get entered

The site fetches match results automatically every 30 minutes from a public data source. If that ever falls behind or breaks, an admin can enter scores manually so the leaderboard stays current.
