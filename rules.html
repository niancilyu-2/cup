How wildcards get slotted into the Round of 32

Eight of the twelve third-place finishers advance. Which third-place team plays whom in the R32 depends on which eight groups contribute a wildcard, not on the teams themselves — FIFA pre-computed every combination into a fixed lookup table called Annexe C.

There are C(12,8) = 495 possible combinations of contributing groups. Annexe C lists all 495, and for each combination it spells out which third-place team is paired with which group-winner / group-runner-up in the Round of 32. The bracket code on this site reads from that same table, so once you've ranked all 12 groups and chosen your 8 wildcards, every R32 slot is filled automatically.

The full text of Annexe C lives in FIFA's official tournament regulations:

What is a good score?

We ran a Monte Carlo simulation with 12,000 simulated player brackets per knowledge level (see below), all scored against one highly informed fake-tournament result permutation.

  • Complete randomness: picks do not use team strength at all.
  • Some football knowledge: picks lean toward stronger teams, but with plenty of miss rate.
  • Expert knowledge: picks follow team strength more closely, with less random noise.
Complete randomness ~18 pts avg Median 17 / 90th pct. 29 / 95th pct. 34. Picks ignore team strength.
Some football knowledge ~43 pts avg Median 42 / 90th pct. 64 / 95th pct. 71. Picks lean toward stronger teams, but still miss plenty.
Expert knowledge ~61 pts avg Median 60 / 90th pct. 80 / 95th pct. 86. Picks have stronger signal and lower pick noise.

Simulation methodology:

  • One football-informed fake tournament was generated first, including group rankings, wildcard groups (A/B/D/E/F/H/J/L), Annexe C placement, and seeded knockout match IDs.
  • Then 12,000 simulated players per scenario were scored against that same fixed fake result.
  • S_team is a latent strength rating. Higher means stronger; it is not pool scoring.
  • For each group ranking, match result, or player pick, the model computes V_team = alpha * S_team + sigma * epsilon.
  • epsilon is random shock. sigma is upset/noise size. The higher V_team ranks higher or wins the match.
  • The fake tournament result uses a football-informed signal, so stronger teams are more likely to advance, but noise still creates upsets.
  • Player knowledge is the signal-to-noise ratio: random picks use alpha = 0; stronger scenarios use higher alpha and lower pick noise.
  • Tiebreakers and the 3rd-place match are excluded from scoring.

Save vs. Submit

Your clicks live in a draft until you commit them:

  • Save my picks writes your current draft to the database. You can keep editing afterward.
  • Submit writes your draft AND marks your picks as final. Editing is disabled until you click Edit picks to unlock again.
  • Both Save and Submit are no-ops if your draft already matches the database.
  • Leaving the site (close tab, refresh, click Rules) with unsaved changes triggers a warning. The modal offers a "Save & continue" shortcut, or you can leave without saving.

The tiebreaker, explained

Your champion is whichever team you've picked to win the Final — you don't choose them separately. The tiebreaker number is your prediction of that champion's average goals per game across every match they play (group stage + knockouts).

If two players finish tied on points, whoever's prediction is closest to the real tournament average for the actual champion wins the tiebreaker. The team you picked doesn't have to be the team that actually wins — we always compare against reality.

Decimals are fine: 2.3, 1.75, etc. A typical World Cup winner averages somewhere between 1.5 and 2.5 goals per game.

How to play

  1. Pick your name (or add yourself) on the home page.
  2. For each of the 12 groups, rank all 4 teams from 1st to 4th by dragging them into your preferred order. Only your 1st and 2nd score group-stage points; your 3rd determines wildcard eligibility.
  3. Pick exactly 8 of the 12 third-place teams to advance as wildcards. The R32 bracket auto-populates from FIFA's official Annexe C lookup table.
  4. Click winners through R32 → R16 → QF → SF → Final, plus the 3rd-place match.
  5. Your champion is whoever you pick to win the Final. In the tiebreaker, predict that champion's average goals per game across the whole tournament.
  6. Click Save my picks as you go (commits your current draft to the database). Click Submit when you're done — finalizes everything until you click Edit.

A few mechanics worth knowing:

  • Group ranks: drag a team row up or down inside its group to change its rank; the other rows shift to make room. Each group starts in a random order, so rank all four yourself.
  • Auto-fill: shuffles all four teams in each empty group so the top three become a random 1st/2nd/3rd. Groups you've already touched are left alone.
  • Wildcards: after every group is ranked, pick exactly 8 of the 12 third-place teams. The R32 matchups are looked up from FIFA's official Annexe C table — you don't place teams into slots yourself.
  • Winner picks: click a team in a match to advance them; click again to un-pick.
  • Bracket cascade: change an upstream winner and downstream slots update. Any later pick whose team is no longer in its match gets dropped — re-pick that round.
